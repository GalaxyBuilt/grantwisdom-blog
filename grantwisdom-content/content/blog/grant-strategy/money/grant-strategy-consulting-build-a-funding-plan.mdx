# Grant Strategy Consulting: Build a Funding Plan That Wins Year After Year

*The difference between organizations that struggle to fund their work and those that consistently attract grants is rarely the quality of their programs. It is almost always the quality of their grant strategy. GrantWisdom's grant strategy consulting services help you build the systems, relationships, and positioning needed to win funding year after year.*

## What Grant Strategy Consulting Delivers

Grant strategy consulting is not grant writing. It's the work that happens before the writing — and that makes the writing dramatically more effective.

A grant strategy engagement with GrantWisdom delivers:

**A Funded Future**: A clear picture of where your funding should come from over the next one to three years, with specific funder targets, projected award sizes, and a realistic path to achieving your funding goals.

**Competitive Intelligence**: An honest assessment of where you are genuinely competitive, where you need to build capacity, and which opportunities are worth pursuing now versus in future cycles.

**Relationship Capital**: A plan for building the funder relationships that produce sustainable funding — with specific actions, timelines, and talking points for each priority relationship.

**Operational Excellence**: Systems and processes that make managing a growing grant portfolio efficient, compliant, and sustainable.

**Strategic Clarity**: A clear organizational narrative — your theory of change, your evidence base, your impact story — that resonates across funders and across grant applications.

## Our Grant Strategy Consulting Services

### Funding Assessment and Audit

Before building a new strategy, understand where you stand. Our funding assessment service provides a comprehensive analysis of your current funding portfolio, including:

- Review of all active grants: alignment, award size, reporting burden, renewal probability, and strategic value
- Analysis of your funder relationships: depth, recency, and trajectory
- Assessment of your grant-seeking capacity: staff, systems, and organizational readiness
- Identification of your most significant funding gaps and risks
- Benchmarking against comparable organizations in your sector and region

The assessment concludes with a clear-eyed diagnostic: here's what's working, here's what isn't, and here are the highest-priority changes that will improve your funding outcomes.

### Comprehensive Grant Strategy Development

For organizations ready to build or rebuild their grant strategy, this engagement provides a complete strategic framework:

**Funder Mapping and Prioritization**: Identification of 30 to 50 potential funders ranked by alignment, accessibility, and award potential. Each funder is profiled with giving priorities, past grantees, decision timelines, and recommended engagement approach.

**Portfolio Design**: A recommended portfolio structure that balances diversification, stability, and growth potential. This includes targets for federal, state, foundation, and corporate funding across program areas.

**Prospect Cultivation Plan**: A funder-by-funder plan for relationship development, including specific cultivation touchpoints, timeline, and recommended communication approach for each priority prospect.

**Application Calendar**: A 12-month grant calendar that sequences applications strategically — balancing workload, staggering deadlines, and prioritizing high-value opportunities.

**Organizational Positioning Brief**: A summary of your organizational brand for grant purposes — your core narrative, your theory of change, your evidence base, and your credibility markers. This brief serves as a reference document for all grant writing.

**Implementation Roadmap**: A phased action plan for implementing your strategy, with milestones, responsibilities, and success metrics.

### Grant Portfolio Optimization

For organizations with existing grant programs that have plateaued, our portfolio optimization service identifies the changes that will unlock new growth:

**Portfolio Analysis**: Deep analysis of your current portfolio to identify patterns — which funder types are most productive, which programs attract the most funding, which relationships have growth potential.

**Diversification Planning**: Identification of new funder categories, geographic markets, or program areas that could expand your funding base.

**Relationship Audit**: Assessment of your current funder relationships and a plan for deepening high-potential partnerships while strategically managing or exiting low-value relationships.

**Competitive Positioning Update**: A review of your current positioning against the current competitive landscape and recommendations for updates to your narrative, evidence base, or program design that will strengthen your competitiveness.

### Federal Funding Strategy

Federal grants represent the largest source of funding but require specialized strategic planning. Our federal funding strategy service covers:

**Agency Mapping**: Identification of all federal agencies whose mission and priorities intersect with your work, with program-level analysis of the most aligned competitive grant opportunities.

**Federal Readiness Assessment**: Review of your organization's current readiness for federal funding — registrations, financial management systems, audit history, indirect cost rate, and compliance infrastructure.

**Federal Funding Roadmap**: A phased plan for building federal funding over three to five years — starting with the most accessible programs and progressively pursuing larger, more complex federal opportunities as your capacity grows.

**Congressional Appropriations Strategy**: For organizations with significant federal funding potential, guidance on engaging your congressional delegation in supporting your access to federal programs.

### Funder Relationship Coaching

For executive directors, development directors, and board members who want to build more effective funder relationships, our coaching service provides:

**Funder Meeting Preparation**: Coaching for upcoming funder meetings, including talking points, key messages, and strategies for advancing the relationship.

**Communication Review**: Review and improvement of your between-cycle funder communications — impact updates, site visit materials, and relationship correspondence.

**Board Engagement in Fundraising**: Guidance on engaging your board in funder cultivation and relationship development, including board member assignment to specific funder relationships.

## Strategic Grant Planning for Specific Contexts

### Startups and Early-Stage Organizations

Organizations in their first three years face a chicken-and-egg challenge: funders want to see a track record, but you can't build a track record without funding. Our startup grant strategy service helps early-stage organizations navigate this challenge:

- Identifying funders who explicitly fund early-stage work and new organizations
- Building a compelling pre-track record narrative that emphasizes leadership expertise, community credibility, and proof-of-concept results
- Sequencing funding applications strategically — starting with more accessible local and demographic-specific grants and building toward larger, more competitive programs
- Investing in organizational infrastructure (audit, insurance, governance) that expands eligibility for larger grants

### Scaling Organizations

Organizations moving from small to mid-size face a different challenge: their program footprint is growing, but their grant infrastructure hasn't kept pace. Our scaling strategy service focuses on:

- Building the systems and staff capacity needed to manage a larger, more complex grant portfolio
- Pursuing larger, multi-year grants that provide stability as operational complexity grows
- Developing an indirect cost rate or de minimis rate election strategy that accurately covers organizational overhead
- Building the evaluation infrastructure needed to compete for competitive federal programs

### Organizations in Funding Transition

Organizations facing the loss of a major funder, a shift in program focus, or a leadership transition need urgent strategic support. Our transition strategy service provides rapid assessment and a clear action plan for stabilizing funding during periods of change.
