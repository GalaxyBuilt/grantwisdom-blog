# Industry Grant Consulting: Targeted Funding Support for Your Sector

*Generic grant advice only goes so far. When your organization operates within a specific industry — healthcare, agriculture, technology, arts, or any other sector — you need grant support that understands your world. Industry-focused grant consulting and research services provide exactly that: deep sector knowledge combined with expert grant strategy.*

## Why Industry Expertise Changes Everything in Grant Seeking

Sector-agnostic grant support has its place — but it has real limitations. A grant writer who doesn't understand healthcare delivery models will struggle to articulate clinical outcomes persuasively. A researcher who doesn't know the USDA program landscape will miss critical agricultural funding. A strategist unfamiliar with the tech innovation funding ecosystem won't know the difference between an SBIR Phase I and a Phase II — or why that difference matters enormously.

Industry grant consulting bridges the gap between general grant knowledge and sector-specific expertise. The result is grant applications that don't just meet requirements — they speak fluently in the language of the funder, reflect deep understanding of the sector's challenges and opportunities, and position your work as a credible, experienced player in the field.

For organizations in specialized sectors, this level of expertise is not a luxury — it is a competitive necessity.

## Healthcare and Life Sciences Grant Services

Healthcare organizations face a uniquely complex grant landscape. Federal funding through NIH, HRSA, SAMHSA, and CMS operates on different rules, timelines, and review criteria than foundation health funding. Navigating this landscape effectively requires sector expertise that spans both program design and funding strategy.

Our healthcare grant services include:

**NIH Proposal Development**: Assistance with specific aims pages, research strategies, biosketches, and the unique formatting requirements of NIH applications. Our team includes writers with experience in R01, R21, K-award, and T32 submissions.

**Community Health Center Funding**: FQHC funding, HRSA look-alike applications, and New Access Point grants require detailed knowledge of UDS data requirements, sliding-fee schedule documentation, and PCMH standards. We provide end-to-end support.

**Behavioral Health and Substance Use**: SAMHSA grant programs, state behavioral health authority contracts, and foundation funding for mental health and substance use treatment require specific clinical and policy knowledge. We bring that knowledge to every proposal.

**Health Equity Initiatives**: With growing funder investment in health equity, community health workers, and social determinants of health, we help organizations frame their health equity work in ways that resonate with both government and foundation funders.

## Technology and Innovation Grant Services

Tech startups and innovation-focused organizations operate in one of the most dynamic — and most opaque — grant funding environments. The SBIR/STTR ecosystem alone is complex enough to require specialized expertise, and that's before accounting for state programs, corporate grants, and foundation innovation funding.

Our technology grant services include:

**SBIR/STTR Application Support**: Phase I and Phase II application development, commercialization planning, and agency-specific strategy. We have experience with NSF, NIH, DOE, DOD, and USDA SBIR programs.

**State Technology Grant Programs**: State economic development agencies and technology commissions offer programs that many startups overlook. We research and pursue state-level opportunities tailored to your location and technology focus.

**Corporate Innovation Grant Programs**: Google.org, the Microsoft AI for Good program, Amazon's AWS Grants, and dozens of corporate foundations fund tech-enabled solutions. We identify the best fits and develop compelling applications aligned with each corporate partner's goals.

**Clean Tech and Energy Innovation**: With the post-IRA surge in clean energy funding, we help tech companies pursuing energy transition, grid technology, EV infrastructure, and climate solutions navigate a rapidly evolving federal grant landscape.

## Agriculture and Food Systems Grant Services

Agricultural grant funding is substantial — but it's also scattered across dozens of USDA programs, state agencies, and niche foundations. Effective agricultural grant seeking requires deep familiarity with program-specific requirements and the agricultural policy context that shapes funder priorities.

Our agriculture grant services include:

**USDA NIFA Grant Applications**: Research proposals for AFRI (Agriculture and Food Research Initiative) and other NIFA competitive programs. These are highly technical applications that require deep agricultural research knowledge.

**Value-Added Producer Grants (VAPG)**: Support for farmers and ranching cooperatives developing value-added products. We help with feasibility studies, business plans, and proposal narratives.

**Sustainable and Organic Agriculture Funding**: Foundation and government funding for sustainable agriculture, soil health, organic certification support, and regenerative practices. We know the funders and we know the language.

**Food Access and Urban Agriculture**: Grants for community gardens, urban farms, food hubs, and food access initiatives from USDA, EPA, and private foundations. We help community-based organizations access funding to build more equitable local food systems.

## Arts and Culture Grant Services

Arts funding has its own unique culture — literally. The relationship between arts organizations and funders is often more personal and relationship-driven than in other sectors. Successful arts grant seeking requires both strong writing and deep knowledge of the arts funding ecosystem.

Our arts and culture grant services include:

**NEA and State Arts Agency Applications**: National Endowment for the Arts grants and state arts agency applications require specific formatting, artistic documentation standards, and community impact frameworks. We develop applications that meet technical requirements while showcasing artistic vision compellingly.

**Foundation Arts Funding**: Major arts foundations like Mellon, Doris Duke, and Kresge have specific program interests and proposal formats. We research fit carefully and craft proposals that reflect genuine alignment with each funder's priorities.

**Individual Artist Grants and Fellowships**: Fellowship applications, artist-in-residence proposals, and individual artist grants require a distinct voice that blends artistic biography with program narrative. Our team works closely with individual artists to capture their voice while meeting funder expectations.

**Cultural Heritage and Humanities**: National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) grants for preservation, public programming, and scholarly projects require specific proposal structures. We support NEH applications across all major program areas.

## Education and Workforce Development Grant Services

Education funding spans a complex landscape from early childhood through workforce development, with federal, state, foundation, and corporate funders each operating with different priorities and requirements.

Our education grant services include:

**Department of Education Grant Applications**: i3/EIR (Education Innovation and Research), Promise Neighborhoods, Full-Service Community Schools, and dozens of other competitive programs. Federal education grants are complex and competitive; we bring the expertise to navigate them effectively.

**Workforce Development and WIOA**: WIOA Title I and Title II programs, Job Corps, apprenticeship grants, and sector-based workforce development initiatives. We understand workforce policy and help organizations develop competitive workforce development proposals.

**Early Childhood Education**: Head Start, Early Head Start, Child Care Development Block Grant, and foundation early childhood funding. We help early childhood organizations navigate both federal program requirements and foundation alignment.

**College Access and Success**: College Gear Up, TRiO programs, and foundation college access funding. We help organizations serving first-generation and underrepresented students make a compelling case for support.
